Double Bag Static Line

What was this course?

"Double Bag Static Line" refers to a configuration of a parachute system. This is a free fall chute that gets packed into an extra bag connected to a static line. This is beneficial for team inserts because the static line system allows everyone's chute to deploy at roughly the same altitude.

My team and I went to this course to learn how to pack, jump, and pilot this rig configuration. One more skillset to add to the repertoire.

Also in this course we learned how to rig and jump full loads of equipment to make this a practical skill that we could actually use to do our jobs.

Reason for Attending

Where the Army's Basic Airborne Course teaches you the basics of parachuting and landing, you are only taught how to do low-level jumps which are not actually practical in a combat environment. Jumping at low altitudes exposes the aircraft to enemy fire and observation, as well as alerts everyone to the fact that you are parachuting in.

This course teaches the use of a free-fall ram-air chute which allows for jumps at higher altitudes. The chute also has a higher glide ratio (meaning you move forwward faster than you fall under canopy) which allows the plane to drop you off further away from enemy lines.

Course Details

Location:
Parker, AZ.
The course was put on by a USMC Mobile Training Team (MTT) at the facilities owned by IDIS Corp. (see IDIS page) so that we could seamlessly finish the DBSL course and begin the IDIS course.
Length:
Approx. 2 weeks
Jumps:
13 jumps, all at 6,500 ft. msl
